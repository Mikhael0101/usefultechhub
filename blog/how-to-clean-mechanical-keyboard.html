How to Clean Your Mechanical Keyboard

January 5, 2026 · 5 min read · By Tech Team

Your mechanical keyboard is probably dirtier than you think. Dust, crumbs, skin oils, and debris accumulate under the keycaps over time, affecting both hygiene and performance. Here's how to give your keyboard a proper deep clean.

What You'll Need

  • Keycap puller (usually included with your keyboard)
  • Compressed air or electric air duster
  • Soft brush (old toothbrush works)
  • Microfiber cloth
  • Isopropyl alcohol (90%+ recommended)
  • Bowl of warm soapy water
  • Optional: Switch lubricant for extra maintenance

Step 1: Unplug and Photograph

First, unplug your keyboard (or turn it off if wireless). Before removing any keycaps, take a photo of your keyboard layout. This is especially important for non-standard layouts—you'll thank yourself later when putting everything back together.

Step 2: Remove the Keycaps

Use a keycap puller to remove all keycaps. Work from the edges inward. For larger keys (spacebar, shift, enter), be careful of the stabilizers underneath. Pull straight up with gentle, even pressure.

Tip: Place keycaps in a bowl or bag so you don't lose any. They're small and love to roll away.

Step 3: Clean the Keycaps

Fill a bowl with warm water and a drop of dish soap. Submerge all keycaps and let them soak for 30-60 minutes. After soaking, gently scrub each keycap with your fingers or a soft brush. Rinse thoroughly and lay them on a towel to dry completely (at least 4-6 hours, overnight is best).

Warning: Never put keycaps back on while wet. Moisture can damage switches and cause corrosion.

Step 4: Clean the Keyboard Base

With keycaps removed, you'll see all the dust and debris that's accumulated. Here's how to clean the base:

  1. Flip and shake – Turn the keyboard upside down and gently shake to dislodge loose debris
  2. Compressed air – Blast between switches to remove remaining dust
  3. Brush – Use a soft brush to sweep debris from around switches
  4. Wipe down – Dampen a microfiber cloth with isopropyl alcohol and wipe the plate and case

Step 5: Reassemble

Once keycaps are completely dry, refer to your photo and press each keycap back onto its switch. Press firmly until you feel/hear a click. Test each key to ensure proper seating.

Maintenance Tips

  • Weekly: Quick wipe-down with microfiber cloth
  • Monthly: Compressed air between keys
  • Every 3-6 months: Full deep clean as described above
  • Always: Wash hands before typing, no eating over keyboard
