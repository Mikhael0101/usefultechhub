Modern laptops are slim, but that often means fewer ports. A good USB-C hub expands your connectivity without breaking the bank. Here's everything you need to know before buying.

What to Look For

1. Port Selection

Consider what you actually need to connect:

  • USB-A ports: For legacy devices, flash drives, mice
  • HDMI/DisplayPort: For external monitors
  • SD card reader: Essential for photographers
  • Ethernet: For reliable wired internet
  • USB-C pass-through: For charging while using the hub
Tip: Don't buy more ports than you need. Each additional port adds cost and potential points of failure.

2. Power Delivery (PD)

If you want to charge your laptop through the hub, check the power delivery rating:

  • 60W: Sufficient for ultrabooks and light laptops
  • 85-100W: Needed for powerful laptops like MacBook Pro 15/16"
Warning: Some cheap hubs advertise PD but don't deliver full power to your laptop. Stick with reputable brands.

3. Build Quality

Aluminum housings dissipate heat better than plastic. This matters because USB-C hubs can get warm under heavy use, and heat affects performance and longevity.

Common Questions

Can I connect multiple monitors?

Most basic hubs support one external display. For multiple monitors, you need a dock with MST (Multi-Stream Transport) support, or use a combination of USB-C and HDMI outputs if your laptop supports it.

Why does my hub get hot?

USB-C hubs transfer a lot of data and power. Some heat is normal, especially during heavy use. Metal-bodied hubs handle this better than plastic ones.

Will any USB-C hub work with my laptop?

Most will work for basic USB and data transfer. For video output, your laptop needs to support DisplayPort Alt Mode over USB-C. Check your laptop specs to be sure.
