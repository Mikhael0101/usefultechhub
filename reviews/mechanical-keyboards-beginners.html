Switch Types Explained (Quick Guide)

Mechanical keyboards use individual switches under each key. Here are the three main types:

Red (Linear)
Smooth keypress, no bump or click. Quiet. Best for: gaming, fast typing, office use.
Brown (Tactile)
Small bump when key activates. Moderate noise. Best for: typing, general use, beginners.
Blue (Clicky)
Bump + audible click sound. Loud. Best for: typing enthusiasts who don't share office space.
Our recommendation for beginners: Start with Brown switches. They offer tactile feedback without being too loud, making them versatile for both typing and gaming.

Layout Sizes Explained

  • Full-size (100%): Everything including numpad. Big, but all keys accessible.
  • TKL / Tenkeyless (87%): No numpad. Great balance of compactness and functionality.
  • 75%: Compact with function row and arrows. Our favorite for productivity.
  • 65%: No function row but keeps arrows. Good middle ground.
  • 60%: Minimal - no arrows, no F-keys. Requires learning key layers.
First keyboard recommendation: TKL or 75% layouts are ideal for beginners. You get space savings without sacrificing frequently-used keys.

What About Gaming?

All keyboards on this list work great for gaming. The "gaming keyboard" marketing is often just that - marketing. What matters more:

  • Switch type: Linear (Red) switches are popular for gaming due to smooth keypresses
  • Polling rate: 1000Hz is standard and sufficient for all but pro esports
  • N-key rollover: All our picks support pressing multiple keys simultaneously
