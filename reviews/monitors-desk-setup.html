Understanding Monitor Specs

Resolution

  • 1080p (1920x1080): Budget-friendly, easier to run at high FPS
  • 1440p (2560x1440): Sweet spot for gaming and productivity
  • 4K (3840x2160): Best for content creation, demanding on GPU

Refresh Rate

  • 60Hz: Fine for office work, not for gaming
  • 144Hz: Noticeable smoothness, great for most gamers
  • 240Hz+: Diminishing returns, competitive FPS players only

Panel Type

  • IPS: Best colors and viewing angles, slightly slower response
  • VA: Best contrast (deep blacks), some ghosting in fast scenes
  • TN: Fastest response, worst colors—mostly outdated now
  • OLED: Perfect blacks, vibrant colors, burn-in risk, expensive
Our recommendation: For most users, a 27" 1440p 144Hz IPS monitor is the sweet spot. Great for both gaming and work without breaking the bank or your GPU.

Desk Setup Priority Order

If you're building a setup on a budget, prioritize in this order:

  1. Monitor — You stare at it all day, biggest impact
  2. Chair — Your back will thank you (see our separate guide)
  3. Monitor arm — Proper height reduces neck strain
  4. Desk light — Reduces eye fatigue significantly
  5. Cable management — Clean desk, clear mind
  6. Desk mat — Nice to have, not essential
