WEBCAMS

What to Look For in a Webcam

  • Resolution: 1080p is the standard. 4K is overkill for most video calls but nice for recording.
  • Frame rate: 30fps is fine for calls. 60fps matters for streaming.
  • Autofocus: Essential. Manual focus webcams are frustrating.
  • Low-light performance: Important for home offices without perfect lighting.
  • Field of view: 70-90 degrees is ideal. Too wide = distorted edges.
Pro tip: Lighting matters more than camera quality. A $50 webcam with good lighting beats a $200 webcam in a dark room.

MICROPHONES

What to Look For in a Microphone

  • Type: USB mics are plug-and-play. XLR mics need an audio interface but offer more flexibility.
  • Polar pattern: Cardioid picks up sound from the front (best for solo use). Omnidirectional picks up everything.
  • Condenser vs Dynamic: Condensers are more sensitive (great for quiet rooms). Dynamics reject background noise better.
  • Sample rate: 48kHz/16-bit is standard and sufficient for most uses.
Common mistake: Buying an expensive mic without considering your room. A $100 mic in a treated room sounds better than a $300 mic in an echoey space.
