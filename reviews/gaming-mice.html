Understanding Gaming Mouse Specs

DPI (Dots Per Inch)

How sensitive the mouse is. Higher isn't better—most pros use 400-1600 DPI. What matters is the sensor's accuracy at your preferred DPI, not the maximum number.

Polling Rate

How often the mouse reports position (in Hz). 1000Hz is standard. 4000Hz+ exists but the difference is imperceptible for most players. Don't pay extra for it.

Weight

Lighter mice (under 80g) allow faster flicks but less control. Heavier mice (90g+) offer stability. Personal preference matters most—there's no "correct" weight.

What actually matters: Shape, weight, and sensor quality. Marketing specs like "25,600 DPI" are meaningless. Focus on how the mouse feels in your hand.

Grip Styles

Palm Grip

Entire hand rests on mouse. Best for larger, ergonomic mice. Most comfortable for long sessions.

Claw Grip

Palm touches back, fingers arched. Balance of control and speed. Medium-sized mice work best.

Fingertip Grip

Only fingertips touch mouse. Maximum agility. Requires smaller, lighter mice.

How to Choose

For FPS Games

Prioritize weight (under 80g) and shape. The G Pro X Superlight 2 is the safe choice; the DeathAdder V3 if you prefer ergonomic shapes.

For MMO/MOBA

Get a mouse with extra buttons. The Naga X is the best balance of buttons and weight.

For General/Casual Gaming

Comfort matters more than specs. The Basilisk V3 or DeathAdder Essential will serve you well without breaking the bank.

On a Budget

The DeathAdder Essential ($25) for wired, G305 ($40) for wireless. Both punch well above their price.
